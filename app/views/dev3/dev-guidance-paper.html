Sending your paper fit note to DWP

To upload your paper fit note, you will need to make a digital copy of it.

If you do not want to do this, you can post it, without a stamp, to:

Freepost DWP ESA 26

How to make a digital copy of your fit note

You can either:

  1. take a photo of your fit note with a digital camera, smartphone or other digital device, or
  2. scan your fit note using a scanner
  3. scan your fit note using a smartphone (not all smartphones can do this so you will need to check yours)

Take a photo of your fit note

  1. Put the fit note on a flat surface, make sure it has no creases or folds and is lying straight on.
  2. It should be well lit and without any shadows.
  3. If your fit note has a data matrix (a black and white square that looks like a QR code), make sure it's clearly visible.
  4. The whole of the fit note should be visible in the photo.

Scan your fit note

  1. Make sure the scanner is connected to a laptop or other device.
  2. Place the fit note text side down on the scanner. If your fit note is 2-sided, you must scan the side which shows your details.
  3. Make sure the fit note is lying straight and there are no creases or folds.
  4. Save the scanned image somewhere that's easy to find later.

Scan your fit note using a smartphone

Check your user manual to find out if your smartphone can do this. If it can, follow the instructions given.

It's important that the digital copy of your fit note is clear so it can be read by our system. If it cannot be read, it could be rejected.

File size

The file size must not be larger than 15MB. If it's larger than 15MB, you will need to follow the instructions on your device to make a smaller version.

To check the file size (these steps may vary for different types of devices):

  1. Find the file on your device and select it.
  2. Select the option for more information or more details.
  3. Look for the file size - it should not be larger than 15MB or 15000kB.

File format

Your fit note must be in one of these formats:

  1. JPG
  2. JPEG
  3. HEIC
  4. HEIF
  5. BMP
  6. PNG
  7. TIFF
  8. PDF

To check the format, look at the last three letters of the file name.

For example:

  1. .pdf is a PDF file
  2. .jpg is a JPG file
